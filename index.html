How To Win Friends and Influence People

Written by Dale Carnegie

Listen to the Audiobook Here

How to Win Friends and Influence People in the Digital Age teaches in being a better person and better understanding other people, you are more likely to “win friends and influence people”. It teaches how to connect with people, be more likeabe and make meaningful relationships. This does not only relate to business or work but life in general.

This book is an adaptation of the original book by Dale Carnegie, “How to Win Friends and Influence People”, written in 1936 at a time where there was no social media, email or mobile phones so communication was limited to face-to-face interactions, telephone and letters. Even so, the foundations of this book still matter to this day.

This book consists of 4 main parts:

  • Essentials of Engagement
  • Six Ways to Make a Lasting Impression
  • How to Merit and Maintain Others' Trust
  • How to Lead Change Without Resistance or Resentment

Each part contains many principles and I chose a select few that I felt were most important.

Essentials of Engagement

Bury your Boomerangs

Whenever you feel the need to criticize, condemn or complain, don’t. This never achieves anything and you lose out on a chance to make a friend.

Affirm What’s Good

We are human and we make mistakes. Rather than putting someone down for making a mistake, we can help them to do better.

Connect with Core Desires

To connect with someone is to relate to them. Showing someone that you can benefit or interest them is the quickest way to make a friend.
